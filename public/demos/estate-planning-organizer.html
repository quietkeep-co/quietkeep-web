Wishes & Instructions

In your own words. These sections carry the things a form cannot: your preferences, your messages, and the small details that spare your family a hard guess. Everything here prints into your Letter of Instruction.

Your healthcare wishes summary sits alongside a formal advance directive; it does not replace one. Your heirloom list is a helpful guide, not a substitute for your will.

Letter of Instruction

This compiles your inventory highlights, where your documents live, your key people, and your wishes into one printable packet: "What My Family Needs to Know." Print it, store it with your documents, and give a copy to the people who need it.

The Letter of Instruction is a personal guide for your family, not a legal document. It does not replace your will, your powers of attorney, or your beneficiary designations. For legal questions, consult a licensed attorney in your state.
